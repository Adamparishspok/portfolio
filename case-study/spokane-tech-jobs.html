Connecting my local
startup community

Overview

Since 2012, I have been involved in my city's startup scene and had a mental database of our people and companies. I wanted to share this knowledge with others, and it grew into what became Spokanetechjobs.com.

Competitor Analysis User Interviews No-Code Dev Product Design

The Problem

"How might we connect our local tech community founders, employees, and companies?"

Sites like Linkedin, CrunchBase, and Indeed don’t give people context of the community a company or job sits in. Because of this, there isn't a way to easily find local tech companies/startups, jobs, talent, and how they are located relative to one another.

To ensure this was a real need, I started talking with different types of people in our community. The pain point that kept coming up the most was jobs—either someone looking for one or a company trying to hire.

Users & Audience

Employees and founders of tech companies located in Spokane, WA.

Based on my first-hand experience of the community I created proto-personas to represent the types of users I envisioned on the platform.

Key Personas

  • Startup Founder – Wants to stay in the know.
  • Investor – Hunting for local companies to invest in.
  • Software Developer – Looking for a new local job.

Map-Centric Discovery

Map Interface Prototype

Experimenting with UI: Most job boards don’t display a map. I made the whole experience focused around location so users could see context.

Building with No-Code

Since this was a personal project, I had to build the app myself. While I’m a confident front-end dev, I used this as an opportunity to learn Bubble.io.

Over the course of 3 months, I was able to go from Figma mocks to a launched product all on my own.

Workflow

The "Chicken and Egg" Solution

STRATEGY

To get the platform jump-started without existing users, I needed a strategy to populate data while incentivizing new signups.

PHASE 1

Hand-Curated Data

I manually inputted the initial company, job, and talent data to create immediate value.

PHASE 2

Crowdsourced Growth

Anyone could post a job or company, but they first had to create their own profile.

RESULT Information gets added automatically by the community.

Outcomes & Lessons

Extremely well received. I started to see a number of people in the community sign up and help contribute data and post jobs. The app was even featured on the bubble.io blog as “App of The Day”.

Additionally, the process of building an application end-to-end even with a no-code tool gave me more insight into the nuances of the product development process.
